## Extended Bio

Md Shahajalal Mahmud is a Bangladesh-based mobile-focused fullstack software engineer specializing in Kotlin, Jetpack Compose, Android architecture, and PostgreSQL-backed backend systems. He is the Founder and Lead Engineer of Appriyo, a software product company where he independently architects, builds, deploys, and maintains production-grade systems — from mobile applications to multi-tenant SaaS backends.

His engineering philosophy, "Systems beat features," was shaped directly by experience: his first repair-shop management app hit hard limits in data consistency, scalability, and UX once real users started depending on it. Rather than patch around the problems, he scrapped the codebase and rebuilt it from scratch, an experience that reshaped how he approaches architecture, technical debt, and production reliability. That rebuilt system became Amar Repair, a multi-tenant SaaS platform with strict tenant isolation, JWT authentication, and RBAC. A similar rebuild happened with his tutor-management prototype, which evolved into Amar Batch, an offline-first Android app built on Room DB.

Alongside his product work at Appriyo, he has delivered freelance projects for real clients (including a full business website and domain/hosting setup for a Los Angeles–based wholesale business), built a TensorFlow Lite–powered on-device fake currency detector (CashGuard AI, a Top 100 finalist at the SOLVIO AI Hackathon 2025), and completed a JPMorgan Chase Forage software engineering simulation building a Kafka + Spring Boot transaction processor.

He is currently a third-year CSE undergraduate at Northern University of Business & Technology Khulna (NUBTK), expected to graduate in 2027, holding a CGPA of 3.90/4.00. His long-term goal is to become a high-level mobile-focused fullstack software engineer with strong engineering fundamentals, capable of competing globally for high-paying software engineering roles.
